Wh

Wh represents the voiceless consonant corresponding with w, and is pronounced by emitting the breath sharply between the lips. It is a mistake to assimilate the sound to that of f in English, though this has become fashionable in recent years with some of the younger Maoris. In some words wh and h are interchangeable, as kohatu, kowhatu; mahiti, mawhiti. In a few words there is confusion between wh and w, but this may be due to the fact that in early works printed in Maori no distinction was made between the two, both being printed as w. Wh is never found in Maori followed by o or u.

Whaka (ii), causative prefix; probably connected with the previous word. 1. Combined with an intransitive verb, an adjective or participle to form an intransitive verb, it signifies a beginning of, or approach to, the action or condition indicated. Po rua hoki a Tukutuku e whakatata ana ki a Paoa, kihai i tata (T. 199). Na ka whakawhiti a Kupe i te moana o Raukawa, a ka whiti ( Then Kupe set about crossing the straits of Raukawa, and crossed over (T. 109).

2. Combined with a noun to form an intransitive verb, it signifies the assumption of the character or form expressed by the noun. Kua whakatangata taua kukupa (T. 16).

3. As a strict causative it may combine with a verb, adjective, participle, or noun to form a transitive verb. Katahi ano a Rupe ka whakakukupa i a ia ( Made himself into a pigeon) (T. 33). Kia nunui te tao, kaua e whakaririkitia ( Don't let them be made small) (T. 118). E Kupe, e, whakahokia mai te waka ki a au ( Make the canoe return to me) (T. 109). Other varieties and shades of meaning will be found under the word to which whaka is prefixed in each case.

Whiti (i). 1. v.i. Cross over, reach the opposite side. Na ka whakawhiti a Kupe i te moana o Raukawa, a ka whiti (T. 109).

2. Change, turn. Me te ao mauru, e whi te whakawhiro kia whiti ki te tonga-paraki (M. 157). Whiti tau rawa ake, ka mate nga kumara. Whiti ra runga, be inverted, be contrary. Ka kiia ko matou ki te moana, na ka whiti ra runga anake, ko ratou ka riro ki te moana.

3. v.t. Pass through. Ka whiti te tae te papa o te iramutu (Pi. 175, 2).

5. n. Sail for a canoe. Ka rere mai te tuakana, tapahia ana te rahiri ka motu, ka whakaarahia te whakawhiti rawhara (M.M. 185).

6. Return present.

7. The carved boss or check at the end of the handle of a mere, to which the tau, or thong, was attached; also applied to a similar boss which was sometimes carved halfway down the handle of a paddle or of a pouwhenua.

Whiti (ii). 1. v.i. Shine. E whiti, e te ra, maene ki te kiri (M. 250). Ka whiti te atarau (M. 396). So ra whiti (generally one word), sunrise, east. Whiti-te-ra, an expression of contempt: The wretch! Upoko! upoko! whiti-te-ra! (M. 72).

3. n. East. Roua ki whiti, roua ki tonga (M. 222). = rawhiti.

Whiti (iii). 1. v.i. Start, be alarmed. Ka whiti to manawa (M. 33). Whitiana taku poho i to karanga. ‖ kowhiti.

2. Spring, leap. Ka kitea te inanga i reira e whiti ana.

3. Awake. Haramai nei, whakaoho rawa i taku moe, kia whiti rere (S. 86). Whakarehu rawa iho he wairua mongina, whiti rere rawa ake ka hewa kei te ao (M. 15). Te pa mai to waha kia whiti rere ake taku mauri ora ki runga (M. xcix).

4. n. Alarm, shock. Ka tumeke, ka mataku, ka pa te whiti ki roto ki tona ngakau.

Whiti (iv). 1. v.t. Relate, recite. Ka hoki mai te tira, ka mea te tangata o te kainga, “Whitia te korero” (P. 104). Used also of reciting karakia.

2. Affect by recitation of a charm, bewitch. Hoatu, whitia ki te Poutama, ki te whiti a Rangahua.

3. n. Verse, division, section of a song or karakia. Kua wareware etahi whiti e enei karakia. ‖ S. ii, 52.
